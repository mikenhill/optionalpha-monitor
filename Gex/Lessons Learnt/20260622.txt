## Why 7500 Pinned on Jun 18 but Failed on Jun 22

The answer is in **four structural differences** that are completely visible in the intraday data.

---

### 1. The Call Volume Story — Reinforcement vs Abandonment

This is the single most important factor.

**Jun 18 — call volume massively overwhelmed puts and grew all day:**
```
0930:  cvol=5,301   pvol=1,030    call:put ratio = 5.1:1
1000:  cvol=17,362  pvol=5,144    ratio = 3.4:1
1200:  cvol=68,488  pvol=19,501   ratio = 3.5:1
1500:  cvol=141,553 pvol=77,184   ratio = 1.8:1 (still call-heavy)
close: cvol=165,110 pvol=85,594   final ratio = 1.9:1
```
Every hour, more calls were being bought/sold at 7500 than puts. This is market participants **piling into the call wall** and market makers **continuously reinvesting in delta hedges** at 7500. The pin became a self-reinforcing magnet. By 1400 the absolute GEX at 7500 had grown from 4.5B to **13.6B** — a 3× increase — because live volume was stacking more GEX at that level throughout the day.

**Jun 22 — put volume overtook calls by 10:30 and never looked back:**
```
0930:  cvol=7,310   pvol=4,029    call:put ratio = 1.8:1 (ok, slightly call-heavy)
1000:  cvol=10,054  pvol=13,217   ratio = 0.76:1 ← PUT FLOW TAKES OVER
1030:  cvol=13,523  pvol=28,658   ratio = 0.47:1 ← puts 2:1 over calls
1100:  cvol=30,754  pvol=32,883   nearly equal
1500:  cvol=121,403 pvol=45,278   calls catch up... but damage done
close: cvol=127,799 pvol=47,577
```
By 10:30 puts were running at **2× the call rate** at 7500. This means traders were **buying protection / directional puts through the 7500 strike**, forcing market makers to sell futures to hedge those puts — pushing price away from 7500, not toward it.

---

### 2. The Net GEX Regime Flip — Stabiliser vs Accelerator

**Jun 18 — net GEX oscillated but was mostly positive:**
```
0930: +1.46B  →  1000: -3.35B  →  1030: -1.37B  →  1100: +1.77B
1200: +0.13B  →  1230: +0.70B  →  1300: +0.90B  →  1400: +2.98B  →  close: +0.90B
```
After the 10:00 dip into negative, the window net GEX recovered and **stayed positive from 11am onward**. Positive gamma means market makers buy dips and sell rallies — they actively pushed price back toward 7500 whenever it strayed. This is why price (which opened at 7420 after the prior day's crash) steadily climbed back to 7500 and sat there from 1:00pm onward.

**Jun 22 — net GEX went deeply negative at 10:30 and stayed there all day:**
```
0930: +0.82B  →  1000: +4.18B  →  1030: -2.51B ← REGIME FLIP
1100: -6.41B  →  1130: -6.59B  →  1200: -6.20B  →  1300: -5.97B
1400: -6.30B  →  1430: -6.70B  →  1500: -9.39B  →  1530: -9.36B
```
From 10:30 onward the window net GEX was **persistently −6 to −9B**. In negative gamma, market makers sell into falls and buy into rallies — they **amplify** the move away from 7500, not dampen it. Once price broke through 7500 to 7495, the hedging flow pushed it further down, not back up. The mechanism that created the pin on Jun 18 was working **in reverse** on Jun 22.

---

### 3. The GEX at 7500 Itself — Growing vs Collapsing

**Jun 18** — the absolute GEX at 7500 grew continuously as volume accumulated:
```
0930: 4.52B → 1100: 6.65B → 1300: 10.63B → 1400: 13.63B → 1500: 16.75B
```
The more volume transacted at 7500, the **stronger the gravitational pull** became. This is the self-reinforcing pin mechanism described in the transcripts.

**Jun 22** — the absolute GEX at 7500 started to **collapse in the afternoon:**
```
0930: 5.02B → 1000: 4.49B → 1100: 3.96B → ... → 1500: 1.96B → 1530: 0.99B
```
By the close, 7500's GEX had **shrunk to less than 1B from 5B at the open** — less than 20% of its starting magnitude. The pin was dissolving, not strengthening. Traders were closing/rolling their 7500 positions out, removing the gravitational anchor.

---

### 4. The OI Character — Call Wall vs Balanced Pin

| Date | key_call_oi | key_put_oi | key_net_oi | key_vol_net (EOD) | Character |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Jun 18 | 6,192 | 4,518 | **+1,674** | **+78,673** | CALL WALL — call-heavy structure, massive call flow reinforcing |
| Jun 22 | 4,258 | 4,853 | **−595** | ~+80,000 (calls by EOD) | PIN attempt — balanced OI, but put flow dominated mid-session |

Jun 18's 7500 was a **call wall** — large call OI, large call volume, positive net GEX. Market makers were delta-hedging calls by selling above 7500, creating a ceiling that also acted as a gravitational attractor from below. The mechanism was structural.

Jun 22's 7500 had a marginally put-heavy OI (−595) and the put-heavy flow from 10:00–11:00 established a **negative gamma cascade** away from the strike. The pin structure existed in the morning but was dissolved by 10:30 when the put volume torrent broke the balance.
