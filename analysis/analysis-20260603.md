## How to Trade Options Using GEX (Teaching Points Summary)

- **Identify the key strike** — find the absolute GEX outlier (largest magnitude bar). This is the day's anchor level.
- **Classify the level** using call vs put GEX:
  - *Balanced call+put* → **Pin/Magnet**: price oscillates around it; trade iron butterflies/condors
  - *Call-dominated above price* → **Wall/Resistance**: expect rejection; use put spreads or short call spreads
  - *Put-dominated below price* → **Pillar/Support**: expect rebound; use call spreads or short put spreads
  - *Put GEX spread across many strikes* → **Slide risk**: fast directional move, danger of whipsaw
- **Validate with OI and volume** — trust the level only when OI AND same-day volume both concentrate at the same strike
- **Wait for price to stretch** beyond the level before entering — don't chase immediately
- **Take profits quickly** — 0DTE scalps; thesis is short-term reversion, not a trend
- **Positive net GEX / high sentiment** → dampening/stabilising environment; favour range-bound trades
- **Negative net GEX** → amplifying/acceleration environment; expect exaggerated moves, avoid fading momentum
- **Check tomorrow's profile** — if tomorrow's key strike differs, today's pin may drift late in session
- **Avoid or trade with caution on**: FOMC, triple witching, end-of-month/quarter, major news days

### Strongly Negative GEX / GEX Slide — Acceleration Risk

With net GEX at -10.9B, sentiment at 32.5%, and negative gamma distributed across 27 of 40 strikes, this is a **GEX slide** environment. The teaching material describes this as fast directional movement with hedging occurring at multiple levels — sharp moves in both directions with whipsaw risk.

**Possible educational trade logic:**
- Primary thesis is continued downside acceleration. A **long put spread** below current price (e.g. buy 7560 put / sell 7540 put) is consistent with the negative gamma slide setup.
- 7580 sits above price as a **put-heavy resistance** — a **short call spread** (e.g. sell 7580 call / buy 7600 call) on any failed rally back toward 7580 is a secondary idea.
- Avoid fading the move with bullish trades — in a negative gamma slide, bounces can be short-lived and reversals are high risk.

**Invalidation conditions:**
- A strong reclaim of 7580 backed by volume and a shift in the call/put balance would invalidate the bearish setup.
- If put volume at 7580 unwinds rapidly, hedging pressure may be exhausted and a short squeeze is possible.
- A GEX slide can produce sudden violent reversals — if price spikes back through 7580 aggressively, reduce or close short exposure immediately.
